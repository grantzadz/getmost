Why Choose Us?

Choosing the right educational institution is a significant decision that shapes not only academic success but also personal and spiritual growth. At the Nigerian Christian Institute (NCI), we offer a unique and enriching educational experience which sets up the child for academic exploits as well as enhanced personal and spiritual growth.

OUR VISION

To become a leading learning and teaching environment, guided by Christian principles to prepare young people for excellence in life, here and in the hereafter.

OUR MISSION

Providing a world-class learning environment which will prepare youngsters academically, morally, socially and psychologically for success in life and through eternity.

Our Core Values

Excellence, Responsibility and Accountability, Discipline, Diligence, Teamwork, Spirituality

Our Core Values serve as the foundation of our institute, guiding our actions, decisions, and interactions within the NCI community.

Christ Centred Education

At the NCI, education goes beyond textbooks and exams. While emphasizing academic excellence, our daily routine highlights our commitment to providing a Christ-centred academic environment.

Legacy of Academic excellence

With a history dating back to the establishment of the School of Preaching in 1982, NCI has over the years garnered a well-earned reputation for academic excellence.

Beyond Academics changed to World Class Facilities

From the NCI Administrative Block, Science Building, and Lace Building to the Medical Centre, School Chapel, and Eno Otoyo Students' Centre, our campus provides a top class facilities and a conducive environment for academic pursuits, artistic expression, spiritual reflection, and community engagement.

Nigerian Christian Institute

A message from the President

Mr Ekemini Mkpong

The Nigerian Christian Institute (NCI) is a group of educational institutions established by Obong (Dr.) Okon Effiong Mkpong to provide quality Christian education in the South-South region of Nigeria. The journey to the NCI began in January 1982, on the foundation of the School of Preaching, which was initially situated at 123 Ikot Ekpene Road, Uyo.

In January 1988, the Secondary School was approved and started operations with less than fifty students; and by May 1993, the pioneer SS3 class registered and sat for the May/June West African Senior Secondary Certificate Examinations (WASSCE). But the NCI secondary school has grown by leaps and bounds since 1988. From its pioneer students, the school boasts a current population of one hundred and fifty staff, who are divided into eight academic departments, six non-academic units and the medical centre: the Departments of English and Arts, Mathematics, Life Sciences, Social Sciences, Home Economics…
